What “Plaud-like” means for Lexie
  • Shape: rounded rectangle “card” — fits a small pocket or pencil case.
  • Placement: lives beside the open book on a stable surface (table, lap desk, or board). Optional silicone feet or a printed shallow tray so it does not skate — no magnets in the v1 story.
  • Interaction: one obvious press-and-hold; LED on top edge; speaker on front; mic in its own band — same server pipeline as today.
  • Companion feel: pocket → same spot next to the book every reading session — ritual without attaching anything to the pages.
Thickness (card depth) — v1 envelope: Lexie v1 targets ≤ 8 mm total thickness (builder-approved). The WX-024 dev BOM (breakout boards + 503040 5 mm cell) often lands ~9–11 mm until you move to a thinner LiPo, single rigid PCB (SMD mic / amp), and minimal shell — see §1b below. “Plaud-like” means credit-card footprint + pocket + table placement, not matching Plaud’s ~3 mm custom stack.

2 — Where it sits during reading (no clip, no magnets)

Child workflow: book open → Lexie comes out of pocket → set down in the usual spot beside the spread (or in the tray) → press-and-hold → hear answer → leave there or put away. No snap, no steel, no MagSafe — only **gravity + friction** (and optional tray / silicone bumps) keep it put.

5 — Safety (v1 — no magnets)

Skipping magnets avoids swallowed-magnet hazards entirely. Still plan for: battery enclosure integrity (no pouch puncture), no small loose parts after drops, rounded shell, and USB-C port strain relief. Any future magnet idea should be a separate design review.
