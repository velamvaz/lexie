Lexie WX-027 — Beginner bench guide

Work item: WX-027 (Solder prep + safe power documentation)
Multimeter: MS8332C (Commercial Electric) — written for someone who has never used a multimeter before.

Critical: Do not plug the LiPo into the XIAO until you finish the multimeter polarity steps and they match Seeed’s battery guidance. Wrong polarity can damage the board.

What you will accomplish

  1. Solder headers on MAX98357 and INMP441 (and optionally XIAO).
  2. Solder the speaker terminal block on the amp and connect the speaker wires only to the amp.
  3. Use the MS8332C to measure which battery wire is + and which is −, write it down, then plug JST only when it matches the XIAO.

Seeed reference: XIAO ESP32S3 Getting Started (find the LiPo / battery polarity section on the board).

Parts + header type source: lx-4-path-a-component-kit.md. Row/column layout (machine-readable): lx-4-path-a-bench-layout.md.

Why solder if you have a breadboard?

  • Breadboards need rigid male pins for a reliable connection.
  • Breakouts usually ship with loose header strips — you solder them into the PCB holes.
  • The MAX98357 board typically needs a speaker terminal block soldered on.

This soldering is assembly, not “designing a custom PCB.”

Tools and parts checklist

  • Soldering iron + stand + sponge or brass wool
  • Rosin-core solder
  • MAX98357 + header pins + speaker terminal block
  • INMP441 + header pins
  • Optional: XIAO male headers
  • MS8332C + red and black probes (test leads)
  • LiPo — keep away from the hot iron until polarity is verified

Soldering steps

1 Bench safety

  • Clear workspace; use a non-flammable surface.
  • Turn on the iron; wait until it is fully hot.
  • Keep the LiPo away from the soldering area (heat and stray solder).
  • Have the multimeter ready for later — you will use it before first battery plug-in.

2 MAX98357: solder the pin header

  1. Break or cut a male header strip to match the number of holes on the amp board.
  2. Press the header into the breadboard so the long pins point down into the breadboard and the short side sticks up.
  3. Place the MAX98357 PCB on the pins so every hole seats fully.
  4. Tack one corner pin with a small amount of solder.
  5. Re-check the board is not twisted — it should sit flat and parallel to the breadboard.
  6. Solder every remaining pin with shiny, cone-shaped joints.
  7. Visually inspect: no solder bridges between adjacent pins (no blob connecting two pins).

3 MAX98357: solder the speaker terminal block

  1. Insert the terminal block into its footprint (usually two larger holes).
  2. Tack one pin; ensure the block sits flush against the PCB.
  3. Solder the remaining pin(s).
  4. Gentle tug: the block should not move.

4 INMP441: solder the pin header

Same method as Step 2: breadboard jig → tack one pin → align → solder all → check for bridges.

5 (Optional) XIAO: solder headers

If you want easy breadboard use, solder a male header row on the XIAO. Keep pins straight.

6 Speaker to amp only

Never connect speaker wires to the XIAO LiPo JST. Speaker goes to the MAX98357 terminal block only.
  1. Strip the ends of the speaker pigtail.
  2. Tin the bare wire (a thin coat of solder on the strands).
  3. Insert into SPK+ / SPK− (or the silkscreen labels on your board) on the MAX98357 terminal block.
  4. Tighten the screws; do a light pull test — wires should stay put.

Your multimeter (MS8332C) — parts and safety

The MS8332C is a handheld digital multimeter. Before measuring the battery, learn these parts:

  • Display — shows numbers and sometimes a minus sign, units (V, mV), or symbols.
  • Rotary dial — selects what you are measuring. For WX-027 you need DC voltage only.
  • Jacks (holes at the bottom) — where probe plugs insert. Color matters: black lead almost always goes in COM.
  • Probes — red and black; the metal tips touch the circuit. Only touch insulated handles when measuring live battery wires.
Probe insertion: Push the banana plug on each lead fully into the jack until it feels seated. A loose plug causes wild or zero readings.

Multimeter steps — from zero to polarity reading

7 Insert probes correctly

  1. Power off the meter if it has a separate power button (optional on some models).
  2. Plug the black probe into the jack labeled COM.
  3. Plug the red probe into VΩmA or VΩ — the jack for voltage and resistance. Never use the 10A jack for this test.
  4. Push until each plug seats fully.

8 Select DC voltage on the dial

  1. Rotate the dial to DC voltage (see Figure 4).
  2. If your meter has manual ranges, pick a range above 5 V (for example 20 V DC). A 3.7–4.2 V battery should read clearly.
  3. If the meter is auto-ranging, any DC V position is usually enough.
Common beginner mistake: leaving the dial in current (A) or plugging red into 10A and probing both battery wires can act like a short circuit. For polarity, always use DC volts and VΩmA.

9 Measure the battery (not plugged into XIAO)

The battery must still be unplugged from the XIAO. You only touch the two battery conductors (bare wire, exposed pin, or JST metal if you can reach only the metal safely).

  1. Hold one probe in each hand by the insulated handles.
  2. Touch black to one conductor; touch red to the other.
  3. Read the display (steady hands help):
ReadingMeaning
Positive number (about 3.7–4.2) Red probe is on +, black on − for that orientation.
Negative number (e.g. −3.9) Same battery, opposite assignment: red is on −, black on +.
0.00 or unstable Bad contact, wrong mode, weak probe tip, or dead cell — fix before continuing.

Write down: “Wire color ___ = + / −” and “Wire color ___ = + / −” (or label your JST with tape after you know).

10 Match the XIAO JST, then plug once

  1. Open Seeed’s XIAO ESP32S3 page and confirm which JST pin or pad is + on the board.
  2. Compare to your written table from Step 9.
  3. Only if they agree, plug the JST into the XIAO once. If anything disagrees, stop — fix orientation or wiring before applying power.

WX-027 evidence (for your log)

  • MAX98357 headers soldered
  • MAX98357 speaker block soldered
  • INMP441 headers soldered
  • Speaker wired to amp ± only
  • LiPo polarity documented before first plug

Stop — do not power the LiPo if

  • Red probe was in the 10A jack.
  • Dial was in amps or resistance by mistake.
  • Polarity does not match the XIAO JST expectation.
  • Any solder bridge remains on headers.
